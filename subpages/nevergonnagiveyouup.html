Never Gonna Give You Up by Rick Astley

Rick Astley's 1987 hit is widely considered as one of the greatest songs of the '80s. The memorable tune and flashy music video cemented it as a classic song that is hard to forget. It spread rapidly across the Internet as a prank; somebody would click on a link and be transported to the song's YouTube video instead of whatever they intended to visit. Thus began Rick Astley's greatest legacy: the Rickroll.

The Rickroll is widely considered as the greatest meme of all time, and for good reason. First appreaing in 2004, it is a mutated form of the Duckroll, wherein a user clicks a link and is diverted from their intended destination to a photo of a duck on wheels. The first recorded instance of the Rickroll substitited the trailer of Grand Theft Auto 4 for the song. Since this first instance, the Rickroll has not only cememnted itself as an Internet classic, but it has also caused a resurgence in the popularity of Rick himself, winning the EMA 2008 Best Act of All Time, and other awards. Additionally, the Official Music Video is one of an elite club of songs to surpass the 1 Billion View mark.

The use of the song in memes revolves around the 'roll. Aside from the usual 8- and 16-bit remixes, the medival covers, the anime covers, and the bass boosts (lower your volume), a lot of the meme's humor comes from the endless ways one can play/sing the song, and therefore Rickroll you. See the Big Ben's final chime, the Padre's Petco Park Performance, and the stupid amount of soundfonts you can play the song in. Though the song is almost 40 years old, the meme surrounding it is still thriving, still mutating, still surviving. It is one of the unspoken Rules of the Internet: If it exists, it can be turned into a Rickroll. At this point, almost 20 years after the birth of the meme, we still find new ways to preserve Rick Astley's legacy. You could say that we never gave him up.
