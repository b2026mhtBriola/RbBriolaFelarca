Memes Never Die: Immortalized Songs of the Internet

Fortunate Son by Creedence Clearwater Revival

There are a lot of things that come to mind when mentioning the Vietnam War: the thick jungle, UH-1 Huey helicopters, the orange plumes of napalm, and of course, the incessant use of Fortunate Son.

In 1969, CCR's lead John Fogerty wrote a song expressing his anger at the US involvement Vietnam and the Nixon administration: Fortunate Son. It complains that the children of the rich and powerful, the "fortunate ones", don't suffer the consequences of the war like the commoners. The song's message resonated with the American public, becoming the anthem of the anti-war movement.

It's so synonymous with the anti-war movement that it ended up being the de facto anthem of the Vietnam War. From its incessant use in Vietnam-related shows and movies like Forrest Gump (1994) to its appearance in games like CoD: Black Ops (2010), the use of Fortunate son is an easy way to induce flashbacks to its era.

As is Internet tradition, the song has numerous remixes and covers, such as a Retrowave cover, a Eurobeat track, and the inevitable bass boosted edition.

In memes, the song serves as both a theme song for the Vietnam War and in general, anything involving a helicopter (or similar vehicles), a tropcal environment, or just the ending to something. Additionally, the political commentary of the Star Wars series drop a lot of references to America's wars in Vietnam and Afghanistan, and as such the use of Forunate Son is implied. See Order 66, the Battle of Kashyyk, the Battle of Geonosis, The Resistance (The Force Awakens), and Luke's appearance in The Mandalorian.

War is hell, but at least 'Nam had a good soundtrack.
