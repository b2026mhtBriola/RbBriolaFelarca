Memes Never Die: Immortalized Songs of the Internet

Take On Me by a-ha!

Take On Me was created by Norwegian band a-ha, released in 1984 to great success. The song is remembered for its opening riff and iconic chorus, all playing over a unique semi-animated music video. While its creators initially thought it "too pop" for their liking, they changed their mind after listening to it enough. It's one of those songs you can never get tired of, and one you can never get out of your head.

The song is the basis of numerous remixes and parodies, including Mine Diamonds, a "Sh*ttyfluted" edition, and an Otamatone cover. The iconic status of the song means that basically everybody knows the song, resulting in impromptu performances anywhere where people can mention the lyrics or play the tune. See Among Us, a Discord server, and a match of Minecraft Skyblock. Since it's such a happy song, it's also frequently put over a bunch of friends hanging out. See: bros jogging about Los Angeles, a team of Halo Spartans, and a co-op game of Dark Souls
